Lesson 03 · 5 min

Make actual things

Music, art, apps, videos — the distance between 'I have an idea' and 'I made a thing' has never been shorter. Here's how to cross it.

01

Start embarrassingly small

Don't 'make an album' — make 8 seconds of a beat. Don't 'build an app' — make a button that does one thing. AI is amazing at turning tiny ideas into working first drafts, and tiny wins stack fast.

✦Finished and small beats perfect and imaginary. Every time.
02

You're the director, not the typist

AI generates options; you make the calls. Which version is better? What's missing? What feels off? That judgment is called taste — and it's the one part AI can't do for you.

Typist
“Make me a song.” → accepts whatever comes out
Director
“Darker. Slower intro. Keep the hook, cut verse 2.” → it's yours now
03

Say what AI did. Own what you did.

Used AI for the artwork? Say so. Wrote the melody yourself? Own it loudly. Creators who are honest about their process get trusted — and trust is the whole currency of making things.

✦“AI helped” is a normal sentence now. “AI made it, I said I did” is not.
