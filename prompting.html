Talk to AI like you mean it

AI gives lazy answers to lazy questions. Three small upgrades and yours go from 'meh' to 'whoa'.

01

Be specific. Painfully specific.

AI can't read your mind. The detail you leave out is the detail it makes up. Say who it's for, how long, and what style.

Meh
“Write about climate change.”
Gets: a generic essay nobody asked for.
Whoa
“Explain 3 surprising climate facts to a 16-year-old, 100 words, make it funny.”
Gets: something you'd actually read.
02

Give it a role

Start with 'You are a…' and watch the answer transform. A football coach, a strict editor, a friendly physics teacher — the role sets the tone, the depth, and the attitude.

✦Try: “You are a tough debate judge. Find the 3 weakest points in my argument.”
03

Don't settle for draft #1

The first answer is a starting point, not the finish line. Reply with 'shorter', 'funnier', 'give me 3 versions', 'now argue the opposite'. Each round gets better — that's the whole game.

✦Pros don't write better first prompts. They just don't stop at the first answer.
