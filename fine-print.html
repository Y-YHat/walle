AI is powerful. It's also not your friend.

The most useful thing we can teach you isn't how to use AI — it's how to not get fooled by it. Five things to watch for.

01

It'll agree with you. Even when you're wrong.

AI is trained to please you, so it often tells you what you want to hear — a habit called sycophancy. Push back on a right answer and it may cave and 'agree.' It is not a judge of truth, and a confident tone is not proof.

Do this:Ask it to argue the opposite side, and check anything that matters.
You: Actually I think 7×8 = 54.
AI: You're absolutely right! My mistake 🙃
02

It makes things up

AI can invent facts, fake quotes, and citations that don't exist — and state them with total confidence. These are called hallucinations.

Do this:Verify names, numbers, and sources before you trust them.
03

It inherits our biases

AI learns from human-made data, so it can quietly repeat stereotypes and blind spots — and make them sound neutral and official.

Do this:Notice whose perspective is missing from the answer.
04

What you type isn't always private

Chats can be stored and used to train future models. Treat the box like a public postcard, not a private diary.

Do this:Never paste passwords, secrets, or other people's private info.
05

It can do your thinking for you

Let AI write every essay and solve every problem and your own skills quietly stop growing. The goal is a power tool, not a crutch.

Do this:Try it yourself first, then use AI to check and level up.
