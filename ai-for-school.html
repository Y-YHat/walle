Use it for school, honestly

There's a version of using AI that makes you smarter and a version that makes you dumber — and they look almost identical from the outside. Here's the difference.

01

The tutor move, not the ghostwriter move

Same tool, opposite outcomes. Ask AI to write your essay and you've learned nothing (and it usually shows). Ask it to explain, quiz you, and poke holes in YOUR draft — now you have a 24/7 personal tutor.

Ghostwriter
“Write my history essay about the Bulgarian Revival.”
You hand in words you don't understand.
Tutor
“Here's my draft. What's my weakest argument? What would a teacher ask me?”
Your essay, leveled up. Your brain, also.
02

Study modes that actually work

Three magic phrases: “Quiz me on this until I get 5 in a row.” “Explain it like I'm 12, then like I'm 18.” “I'll explain it back to you — tell me what I got wrong.” That last one is the strongest learning trick known to science.

✦Before a test: paste your notes and ask for the 10 questions most likely to come up.
03

Know where the line is

Every school's rules differ, but the honest test is simple: could you explain and defend every sentence you handed in? If yes, AI was a tool. If no, it was a stand-in — and stand-ins get noticed.

✦The goal isn't to never use AI. It's to always be the smartest thing in the conversation.
